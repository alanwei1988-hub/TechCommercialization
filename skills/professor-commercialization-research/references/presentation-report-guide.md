# 商业化分析 PPT Guide

Use this guide to create a management-consulting-style commercialization deck that is both MBA-readable and suitable for direct sharing with the named scholar. The core report is a decision document, not a compressed literature review or a technical-transfer checklist.

## Contents

2. Consulting logic model

## 1. Communication job and reader

Before planning slides, write one internal sentence:

> By the end, the reader should understand who the scholar is, how the research program has evolved and influenced the field, which research themes deserve commercial attention, whether to invest more time, why a customer might pay, what could invalidate the case, and which evidence will decide the next step.

Assume the reader:

- has MBA-level familiarity with customers, strategy, competition, economics, execution, and risk;
- does not know the scholar's algorithms, instruments, field-specific benchmarks, or research vocabulary;
- wants a recommendation with boundaries, not a celebration of academic achievement;
- will challenge weak demand evidence, vague differentiation, invented market size, and unsupported price assumptions.

Treat this reader model as internal design guidance. Unless the user requests an internal-only memo or names another recipient, write every visible string so the finished deck can be forwarded unchanged to the scholar.

The core report serves the commercial decision. It may also support a scholar meeting, but do not turn the core narrative into an interview questionnaire. Keep the full scholar-question set in the appendix or speaker notes. The final core slide should state the decision requested and at most two or three questions that materially change it.

Use the visible artifact name **商业化分析 PPT**. For Chinese output, use `<institution>_<scholar>_商业化分析.pptx` and a neutral headline such as `<姓名><已核验职称>科研成果商业化机会初步分析`. Keep the cover free of the final recommendation so the reader first receives the scholar whole picture on slides 2-3; introduce commercial prioritization on slide 4 and the recommendation on slide 5.

### 1.1 Direct-send cover contract

The cover identifies the subject and the file; it does not explain the production logic.

- Show the scholar, verified title or affiliation, analysis scope, evidence date, and optionally `基于公开资料整理｜供交流讨论`.
- Use a scope subtitle such as `研究全景、商业化方向与下一步验证建议`.
- Do not show a recommendation, reader persona, prompt language, methodology narration, or phrases such as `先看清`, `哪些成果值得`, `进入付费验证`, or `面向 MBA 毕业生`.
- Use `初步分析` when the opportunity set or customer evidence is not established; do not use `机会全景` merely to sound comprehensive.
- Keep at most one contextual metric on the cover. Separate value, unit, label, period, and method. Never depend on a large mixed token such as `19+` fitting a narrow box; prefer `19` plus `年以上`, or give the token at least 15% native width headroom.

## 2. Consulting logic model

### 2.1 Build a governing thought

Write one complete recommendation before writing slide titles. It must state:

- what to do now;
- for which customer and problem;
- through which first offer and commercialization route;
- why this option outranks the alternatives;
- what evidence could still reverse the recommendation.

If this cannot be stated in two plain sentences, the analysis is not ready for slide production.

### 2.2 Use top-down logic

Apply three tests:

1. **Vertical logic**: the body of each slide proves or qualifies its title.
2. **Horizontal logic**: sibling reasons are at the same level, mutually distinct, and collectively sufficient for the point above them.
3. **Story-spine logic**: reading only the titles reproduces the complete recommendation, evidence chain, and action.

Group the core story into six business questions:

1. **Who is this scholar and what has the research program become?** Verified identity/title, field, duration, themes, evolution, representative work, and academic influence.
2. **Which research themes deserve commercial attention?** A clear bridge from the whole research picture to no more than three commercial hypotheses.
3. **What should we do?** Recommendation and boundaries.
4. **Why would a customer care?** Pain, buyer, current solution, timing, and accessible market.
5. **How can this team win and make money?** Offer, value, proof boundary, alternatives, economics, route, pilot, team, and IP.
6. **What must be proven next?** Kill risks, 90-day tests, and decision requested.

Use research themes and chronology only for the concise opening whole-picture map. Do not organize the rest of the core deck by publication year, laboratory method, patent category, or paper taxonomy; those are evidence structures, not the management story.

### 2.3 Separate fact, meaning, and action

For every evidence-bearing slide, distinguish internally:

- **Fact**: what is observed or measured.
- **Meaning**: why it matters commercially.
- **Action**: what the reader should do differently because of it.

Visible copy may combine the three naturally; it must not list facts without a commercial implication.

## 3. Standard core story

Start with the following 21-slide skeleton, including the cover. A concise case may combine adjacent jobs and finish in 18–20 slides. A regulated, hardware, clinical, or manufacturing case may require more than 22. Split whenever necessary; do not compress several narrative jobs into dashboards or small text.

| Slide | Management question | Required answer | Example action-title pattern |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | What decision does this report support? | Scholar, verified title or institution, scope, evidence date, and external-document qualifier when appropriate. | `[姓名][职称]科研成果商业化机会初步分析` |
| 2 | Who is the scholar? | Verified current title/role, field, active research years, publication and citation indicators, representative work, and influence summary. Every metric names its database and retrieval date and explains its plain-language meaning on the same slide. | `[学者]已持续深耕[领域]约[X]年，[指标]在普通语言中意味着[事实]` |
| 3 | What is the whole research picture? | Three to five plain-language themes, active periods, representative contributions, evolution, and how the themes relate. | `研究从[起点]逐步延伸到[当前重点]，形成三条相互关联的主线` |
| 4 | Which research themes deserve commercial attention? | Compare no more than three customer-problem hypotheses. For each, state what the product or service is, what the first payment buys, who buys and what decision changes, and the relative validation judgment with its largest evidence gap. | `[方向A]目前最接近明确买家和可验证交付；其余方向仍缺商业证据` |
| 5 | What is the answer? | One recommendation, three reasons, the largest uncertainty, and the immediate 90-day move. If no option is supportable, state that directly and name the evidence needed. | `建议先向[客户]出售[交付物]；若证据不足，则写“当前不应指定首选方向”` |
| 6 | What are the boundaries? | What to do now, what not to do, and what milestone would justify more investment. | `当前只应验证[窄场景]；在[证据]出现前不应建设通用平台` |
| 7 | Why now? | Two or three customer, regulatory, cost, competitive, or technology shifts that create a timely opening. | `[外部变化]正在把[问题]从“可以改善”变成“必须解决”` |
| 8 | Who has the pain and who pays? | Specific beachhead customer, user, decision maker, budget owner, and purchase trigger. | `[细分客户]应作为首批客户：问题高频、预算明确、试点边界清晰` |
| 9 | How is the problem handled today? | Current workflow, manual/self-build/vendor alternatives, why they are tolerated, and their cost, delay, risk, or quality loss. | `客户目前依赖[现状]，主要代价是[成本、延误或风险]` |
| 10 | Is the first market reachable? | Bottom-up customer pool, budget unit, procurement route, and accessible channels; avoid generic TAM-first claims. | `首批可触达的是约[X]家[客户]，比泛行业市场规模更适合验证` |
| 11 | What is the first sellable offer? | Buyer, scenario, input, activity, complete deliverable, outcome, and explicit exclusions. | `第一笔钱应购买[报告/模块/服务]，而不是一套通用平台` |
| 12 | How does the offer create value in the customer workflow? | Before/after workflow, decision point, success measure, switching burden, and value in revenue, cost, time, risk, or experience. | `[交付物]嵌入[决策点]即可产生价值，无需替换客户核心系统` |
| 13 | Why buy instead of using the current alternative? | Compare the three or four alternatives that matter on outcome, cost, speed, deployment burden, trust, and ownership. | `客户采购的核心理由是[更快/更准/更可信]，而不是技术更新颖` |
| 14 | What is proven and what is not? | `已证明`, `尚未证明`, and `下一项验证`; show the paper-to-product boundary without method-heavy exposition. | `现有研究证明方法可行，但尚未证明其能在客户现场稳定交付` |
| 15 | Why can this team win? | Reuse the opening research picture to identify two or three customer-relevant, hard-to-copy assets and the largest weakness. Citation counts and prestige alone do not qualify. | `真正优势是[数据/场景/信任/know-how]，而非论文数量` |
| 16 | Can the first business be economically sensible and repeatable? | Pricing unit, delivery effort, sales cycle, repeatability, customization burden, and margin logic. | `首单应按项目收费；只有交付工时下降后，订阅模式才成立` |
| 17 | Who should carry it to market? | Compare service, licensing, joint development, component supply, standards/audit, and startup routes; state the chosen route and upgrade trigger. | `先以[服务/联合开发/许可]取得证据，公司化应等到[里程碑]后` |
| 18 | What will the first paid pilot buy? | Named customer type, scope, deliverable, period, price hypothesis, success measure, exclusions, and evidence produced. | `首个付费试点只验证[需求和结果]，不承担[平台化或全面效果]承诺` |
| 19 | What must be in place to execute? | The three most important product, data, team, IP, university-transfer, regulatory, or governance gaps and owners. | `未来90天只需补齐三项条件：[商业负责人]、[交付能力]和[权属路径]` |
| 20 | What would stop the project? | Two or three thesis-killing assumptions, observable signals, test, owner, and stop/change threshold. | `若[需求、现场效果或权属]任一不成立，应停止或更换方向` |
| 21 | What is the decision and next move? | A bounded 30/60/90-day validation plan, resources requested, decision date, continue/stop criteria, and at most three questions that change the decision. | `建议批准一个有边界的90天验证，并在[日期]依据三项证据决定继续或停止` |

Rules for adapting the skeleton:

- Keep slides 2-3 as a concise whole-picture orientation, not a CV or literature review. Move honors lists, full publication history, and detailed methods to the appendix or Excel.
- Put the research-to-commercial prioritization on slide 4 and the recommendation on slide 5 at the latest. Do not insert a contents page or methodology section before them.
- Keep the customer problem and payment logic before detailed method, paper, or architecture explanation. The concise opening research map is the only exception.
- For peer influence, compare scholars only when field, career stage, database, and retrieval date are reasonably aligned. Otherwise show absolute indicators and representative works with a limitation instead of a ranking.
- Treat citations, h-index, publication count, and honors as academic-context evidence, never as proof that customers will pay.
- Combine slides only when each answer remains complete and readable. Do not create a 12-field executive dashboard.
- Split a slide when it contains more than one governing thought, more than one main visual, a long paragraph, or more than three supporting messages.
- If no reliable price, market size, or demand signal exists, show the missing evidence and the test—not invented precision.
- After a long or complex core deck, repeat the decision on the final slide rather than ending with a generic `谢谢`.

### 3.1 Opportunity-screening slide contract

Slide 4 prioritizes hypotheses; it is not the final recommendation page. Use no more than three mutually distinct directions and make every row answer four questions:

1. `方向是什么`: concrete product or service form plus the customer result, not an algorithm, dataset, instrument, or paper title.
2. `第一笔钱买什么`: paid unit or period plus a tangible report, test, module, license, sample, or service. `平台`, `解决方案`, `算法能力`, and `联合研究` are insufficient without a bounded deliverable.
3. `卖给谁 / 改变什么决策`: economic buyer or budget-owning role plus the launch, purchase, renewal, iteration, or stop decision.
4. `验证优先级 / 判断`: a Chinese status, evidence confidence or largest missing proof, and the next validation. Use `买方假设` when no buyer has been directly validated.

A visible score means relative validation priority among the options in this report. It is not market size, revenue, success probability, technical maturity, return, or proof that customers will pay. Use whole numbers only, pair the score with a written judgment and evidence gap, and let any hard blocker override the score. If the evidence cannot support a meaningful score, show `需要验证` instead of invented precision.

## 4. Appendix structure

Use the appendix for evidence and diligence detail that supports, but does not drive, the management story:

- Scope, identity disambiguation, and data completeness.
- Full research-direction map and publication timeline.
- Representative papers grouped by claim, including findings and limits.
- Technical principles, architecture, benchmarks, and evaluation methods.
- Opportunity-scoring dimensions, weights, calculations, and assumptions.
- Bottom-up market-sizing details and sensitivity cases.
- Detailed competitors, substitutes, and source list.
- Patents, software, data rights, open-source licensing, and university-transfer facts.
- Full execution roles, governance, conflicts, and regulatory checklist.
- Complete 30/60/90-day workplan.
- Six to eight prioritized scholar questions plus natural follow-ups.
- Counterevidence, unresolved facts, and source/evidence mapping.

Keep the complete paper inventory in the 论文列表 Excel. Do not reproduce the workbook as appendix slides.

## 5. Page grammar

Every core slide uses the same reasoning grammar, without forcing the same visual layout:

1. **Action title**: one complete conclusion, normally no more than two lines.
2. **Evidence body**: one main visual, comparison, process, or number group plus no more than three supporting messages.
3. **Decision implication**: a clear `因此` statement, next action, or boundary when it is not already explicit in the title.
4. **Source footer**: evidence IDs, date, scope, assumptions, and limitations.

Additional rules:

- Use one main visual or comparison per slide. If two visuals tell different stories, split the slide.
- Body copy should normally be 2–4 short evidence statements. Avoid continuous paragraphs longer than two displayed lines.
- Do not repeat the title in the body. Use the body to prove, quantify, qualify, or operationalize it.
- Do not use six to eight equal-sized cards to imply structure. Build a sequence, comparison, or hierarchy instead.
- Use tables only when exact row/column comparison changes the decision. Keep each cell short and split dense comparisons.
- Keep citations legible but visually subordinate. Show compact `Pxx/Sxx` IDs; keep full URLs in Excel.
- Each number must have a unit, period, comparison base, and business meaning. Estimates must show their assumption or range.

### 5.1 Opportunity-table geometry

- Use identical frame geometry by column across comparable rows: the same x-position, relative y-position, width, height, type scale, alignment, and vertical alignment.
- Use a shared two-level vertical grid. Rank, direction title, buyer, score, and status occupy the primary band; direction explanation and changed decision occupy the secondary band. The first-paid-deliverable cell may span both bands and remains vertically centered.
- Vertically center repeated-row text groups. Do not top-align one line inside a box substantially taller than one line; the invisible bottom whitespace makes the row appear top-heavy.
- Author line breaks at semantic boundaries and plan a stable line count. Never allow ranges, units, buyer roles, deliverable names, technical terms, or qualifiers such as `若重启：` to split accidentally. A stranded line such as `6 至 10` fails.
- Highlight at most one direction. Status must be written in words; color is supplementary.
- Use at most one full-width conclusion band. If the slide already has a standard `本页结论` band, do not stack another band that repeats it.

## 6. Action-title rules

### 6.1 Required title form

Use a complete declarative sentence containing a judgment, comparison, cause, boundary, or action. Prefer the pattern:

> 主体 + 关键判断或变化 + 对商业决策的含义

Examples:

- `人因模型评测兼顾买家清晰度和交付可行性，应作为首个切入口`
- `现有代码足以支持联合试验，但尚不能直接交给客户自助使用`
- `先按项目收费更符合当前采购路径，重复需求出现后再考虑订阅`
- `跨客户稳定性和数据权利是立项前必须关闭的两项风险`

Do not use topic labels such as:

- `科研方向概览`
- `市场分析`
- `技术成熟度`
- `竞争格局`
- `商业模式`
- `风险与挑战`

Question titles are acceptable on the cover or a true section divider. Analytical slides must answer the question.

### 6.2 Title-only story test

Export all core-slide titles in order and read them as one memo. The sequence must answer:

1. What should be done?
2. Why does the customer case exist now?
3. Which opportunity wins and why?
4. What is sold and why will the customer buy?
5. Why can this team deliver?
6. How will it make money and reach market?
7. What could stop it?
8. What happens next?

Fail the deck if the title list reads like a table of contents, contains repeated claims, skips a reasoning link, or requires body text to reveal the recommendation.

## 7. Visual and chart rules

Follow the presentations skill in full. Apply these domain-specific constraints:

- Use 16:9 widescreen.
- Use at least 50 pt for the deck title, 35 pt for action titles, 24 pt for mid-level labels, and 18 pt for body text when no template overrides them. Use 16 pt only for short evidence notes, not normal body copy.
- Minimum sizes are hard floors. Add slides or move detail to the appendix instead of reducing type.
- Prefer a flat consulting composition with strong alignment and whitespace over repeated cards, pills, badges, or software-dashboard styling.
- Apply consulting communication principles without copying any firm's logos, proprietary template, branded color system, or distinctive page design.
- Use a restrained palette and one emphasis color. Highlight only the one or two data series that prove the title; mute the rest.
- Use native 2x2s, spectra, workflows, bridges, waterfalls, or timelines only when they clarify the current claim. Avoid decorative frameworks.
- A chart title states the finding; a subtitle states metric, period, and scope. Footnotes state source, sample, assumptions, and limitations.
- Do not chart publication counts, patents, or top-down market size unless the visual directly changes the opportunity decision.
- Avoid generic stock imagery. Use a product mockup, customer workflow, instrument, data flow, or market evidence only when it adds understanding.
- Repeat text labels for status; color cannot be the only encoding.
- Explain unfamiliar metrics next to the number, not only in speaker notes or the appendix. For example, state that Scopus is an academic-literature database, that its document count is the number attributed to the author profile, and that `h=32` means at least 32 works have at least 32 citations each.
- Treat `+`, `%`, `/`, `×`, currency symbols, range signs, and units as part of the numeric token. They must not become the first or only character of a wrapped line. Prefer natural Chinese such as `19 年以上`; otherwise use a non-wrapping shape with width headroom or deliberately separate value and suffix shapes.
- Do not combine multiple metrics such as `2,950 / 32` in a narrow panel unless the label and explanation make both values immediately legible; separate the metrics when space permits.

### Zero-truncation rule

- Do not use `…`, `...`, text clamp, substring slicing, or shortened placeholder text to make content fit.
- Do not allow a text box, table cell, chart label, footer, or speaker prompt to hide characters outside its bounds.
- Do not accept a rendered line that begins with punctuation, contains a one-character orphan, or splits an English word or meaningful Chinese term.
- If a source URL is too long, show a complete `Sxx/Pxx` identifier and keep the full URL in the workbook.
- Any visible layout-created ellipsis is a QA failure.

## 8. Evidence and speaker support

On each evidence-bearing slide:

- Explain the evidence state in natural language such as `已在论文数据中证明`, `已观察到市场信号`, `本报告推断`, or `需要访谈确认`.
- Show relevant `Pxx/Sxx` identifiers in a restrained footer or evidence note.
- Map each major claim to `Selected Evidence` in the workbook.
- Include counterevidence or a known limit when it could change the recommendation.
- Keep confidence separate from commercial attractiveness.

When speaker notes are supported, use them for:

- a concise talk track and transition;
- technical detail useful in Q&A but too dense for the slide;
- exact source context, assumptions, and caveats;
- scholar-question follow-ups and sensitive wording.

Do not put production instructions, hidden scoring debates, or model-facing notes in visible content.

## 9. QA gate

Before delivery:

1. Render every slide to PNG and inspect every slide individually at full size.
2. Inspect a contact sheet for narrative flow and visual rhythm; do not use it as the only review.
3. Run the presentations skill overflow/overlap test and native PowerPoint text-bound checks when available.
4. Fix all clipped text, awkward wraps, broken connectors, inconsistent footers, missing glyphs, and unreadable citations.
5. Confirm slide 1 is neutral and does not reveal the recommendation; confirm slide 2 verifies the scholar's current title, field, active research years, influence indicators, database, retrieval date, and limitations without becoming a CV or honors wall.
6. Confirm the deck passes the direct-send test: the scholar can receive it unchanged, and no visible copy exposes the reader persona, prompt, workflow, QA process, model, tool, or script.
7. Confirm every unfamiliar metric on slide 2 and elsewhere explains what it is, what the number literally means, and what it cannot prove. Prefer plain-language meaning in the title.
8. Confirm slide 3 gives a plain-language whole picture of three to five research themes and their evolution; slide 4 identifies which themes deserve commercial attention; slide 5 states the recommendation.
9. On every opportunity-screening slide, confirm each row states the product or service form, first paid deliverable, buyer and changed decision, and validation judgment with its largest evidence gap. Confirm any score is labeled and worded as relative validation priority.
10. Compare repeated-row coordinates, heights, type sizes, alignment, vertical alignment, and intended line counts by column. Fail unplanned row-to-row geometry differences, top-heavy one-line cells, isolated ranges or units, and accidental line-count patterns.
11. Confirm customer pain, buyer, and current solution appear before detailed method, paper, or architecture exposition.
12. Export and review the title-only story; fail topic labels, logical gaps, duplicated research descriptions, or a recommendation delayed beyond slide 5.
13. Run the `60-second context test`: an MBA-level reader can explain who the scholar is, what they study, for how long, and where their academic influence sits from slides 2-3.
14. Run the `60-second answer test`: the same reader can explain the recommendation, reasons, boundary, and next step from slides 4-5.
15. Run the `30-second slide test`: the reader can state the slide conclusion and implication after one reading.
16. Run the `term-necessity test`: every technical term in the core deck changes a decision and is explained in everyday language.
17. Confirm each recommendation follows `建议 — 依据 — 尚未确认 — 验证动作`.
18. Confirm each risk has an observable signal, owner, test, and stop/change threshold.
19. Confirm each important number has unit, period, comparison base, source, and business meaning. For citations and h-index, also confirm one consistent database and retrieval date.
20. Confirm the core deck contains no full paper inventory, detailed scoring mechanics, or spreadsheet-like wall of cells.
21. Confirm all `Pxx/Sxx` identifiers resolve in the Excel workbook.
22. Search visible text and authoring source for `…`, `...`, `clip`, `slice`, `substring`, and `text-clamp`; inspect every hit.
23. Inspect rendered line starts and endings for stranded punctuation, one-character orphans, and split English or meaningful Chinese terms.
24. Confirm native PowerPoint PNGs and metadata were generated after the final PPT build. For constrained metric panels and opportunity rows, compare native text, actual line count, text bounds, usable shape bounds, and the full-size PNG against the authoring render.
25. Save `slide_qa.tsv`, `slide_qa.csv`, or an equivalent structured log with one row per slide and these columns: `Slide`, `Title`, `Title Story`, `Scholar Context`, `Direct Send`, `Metric Meaning`, `MBA Reader`, `Min Font`, `No Ellipsis/Truncation`, `No Overflow/Overlap`, `Citations Readable`, `Status`, `Fix`. Add `Opportunity Row Complete`, `Score Honesty`, `Row Vertical Rhythm`, `Controlled Wrap`, `Narrow Token Integrity`, and `Native Render Fresh` for opportunity-screening slides.
26. Delivery is blocked unless every row is `PASS`.

Do not deliver a deck that passed only a montage, spellcheck, or layout scan. Logical and ordinary-reader checks are mandatory.
