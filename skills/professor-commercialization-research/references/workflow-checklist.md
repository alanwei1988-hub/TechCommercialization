# Workflow Checklist 2.0

Use this checklist for a full scholar/lab commercialization project. Apply **Decision-first, Evidence-backed** throughout.

## Contents

1. Intake and setup
2. Identity and corpus
3. Capability synthesis
4. Commercial triage
5. Evidence deepening
6. Product and commercialization design
7. Scholar discussion
8. Deliverables and QA

## 1. Intake and setup

- If the scholar and institution are missing, ask: "你想研究哪个机构的哪位学者？如果已有关注时间范围、重点产业方向或需要回答的商业问题，也可以一起告诉我。"
- Capture the target, institution, time window, language, intended decision, and any sector constraint.
- Default to a 10-year window and the two-file package: 商业化分析 PPT plus 论文列表 Excel.
- Create project folders for `metadata`, `papers`, `notes`, `scripts`, and `deliverables`.
- Save machine-readable metadata early so both deliverables can be regenerated.

## 2. Identity and corpus

- Confirm affiliation with official university/lab pages and at least two scholarly indexes when possible.
- Track Chinese/English names, initials, prior affiliations, coauthors, ORCID, and same-name risks.
- Record the verified current title/role, field, first and latest relevant works, active research years, representative work, and dated academic-influence indicators.
- For citation or h-index comparisons, use one named database and retrieval date; compare peers only on a like-for-like field and career-stage basis. Do not mix database counts.
- Build one publication row per work with stable IDs `P01`, `P02`, and so on.
- Deduplicate by DOI, then normalized title plus year/coauthors.
- Keep inclusion/exclusion notes for ambiguous or near-duplicate records.
- Download only lawful open PDFs or author copies; record the source URL and status.
- Preserve stable PDF filenames beginning with the paper ID.

## 3. Capability synthesis

- Give every included paper a compact technical note, plain-language explanation, application scenario, commercial-relevance label, confidence, and known limit.
- Cluster papers by recurring problem, method, workflow, and application domain.
- Rename clusters in buyer-readable language.
- Identify the first key node, strongest evidence, most commercially relevant result, recent pivot, recurring collaborators, datasets, prototypes, patents, open-source assets, or other lab-specific advantages.
- Build a capability map before proposing products.
- Build a concise opening whole-picture map with three to five themes, active periods, representative contributions, and visible evolution; use this to orient the reader before commercial prioritization.

## 4. Commercial triage

- Read `commercialization-framework.md`.
- Compare no more than three opportunities using demand, buyer and budget clarity, productization, differentiation, adoption, economics, and transfer readiness.
- Check current solutions before claiming a market gap.
- Score each opportunity and assign `Go`, `Watch`, or `Avoid`.
- Apply hard blockers separately from the numerical score.
- Select zero or one Lead Wedge. If the evidence is too thin or a hard blocker remains, record `No Responsible Lead Wedge Yet` instead of forcing a recommendation.
- Record `Technical Evidence`, `Market Evidence`, `Inference`, and `Interview Needed` separately.
- Explain why the Lead Wedge deserves priority over the alternatives.

## 5. Evidence deepening

- Select representative, high-commercial-relevance, contradictory, recent-pivot, and user-selected papers for full-text analysis.
- Read abstract, introduction, method, evaluation, conclusion, and limitations where full text is available.
- Test the proposed product claim against what the paper actually demonstrates.
- Classify hypotheses as accurate, partially accurate, inaccurate, or untested.
- Rewrite overbroad hypotheses into defensible product framing.
- Keep the full paper index in Excel; do not turn every paper into a PPT slide.
- After preliminary triage, ask once whether the user wants particular paper numbers deep-dived; continue without blocking if no selection is provided.

## 6. Product and commercialization design

- Define the concrete product form, MVP, core user, workflow, must-have features, success metric, V1 exclusions, and productization gaps.
- Identify user, buyer, budget owner, purchase trigger, current workaround, dissatisfaction, procurement barrier, and build-vs-buy dynamics.
- State a pilot offer and pricing basis when evidence permits; otherwise label them `需要验证`.
- Compare startup, licensing, joint development, service lab, component supply, standards/audit, and open-source-plus-support routes.
- Recommend one commercialization vehicle and explain why it fits better than the alternatives.
- Define the minimum operating team, PI/student roles, external CEO/BD and engineering needs, IP boundary, school-transfer issues, and governance risks.
- Produce 30/60/90-day steps with owner, target, concrete output, validation question, success signal, and failure signal.
- Refresh opportunity scores, confidence, hard blockers, and the recommended direction after evidence deepening and customer, economics, IP, and execution analysis.

## 7. Scholar discussion

- Read `interview-question-guide.md`.
- Convert the most decision-relevant unknowns into natural scholar questions.
- Put at most two or three questions that directly change the requested decision on the final core slide.
- Put six to eight `Must ask` questions and the full prioritized list in appendix slides or speaker notes.
- State what answer would raise, lower, or redirect the recommendation.
- Keep technical paper detail in the evidence basis or follow-up prompt rather than the main question wording.

## 8. Deliverables and QA

- Read `presentation-report-guide.md`, `consulting-language-guide.md`, and `deliverable-schema.md`.
- Create `<institution>_<scholar>_商业化分析.pptx` with the required decision story.
- Create `<institution>_<scholar>_论文列表.xlsx` with `Paper List` first and no more than four default sheets.
- Lock the final PPT order, then populate Excel `Used in PPT Slides` fields.
- Verify paper counts, source IDs, URLs, date range, local paths, judgments, scores, confidence, and cross-file mappings.
- Browse and cite current market, company, product, policy, procurement, and regulatory claims.
- Render and inspect every PPT slide individually; run overflow/overlap checks.
- Inspect and render every Excel sheet; scan formulas for errors.
- Confirm the PPT contains no full paper inventory or spreadsheet-like wall of cells.
- Confirm slides 2-3 establish the scholar's verified identity/title, field, active research years, research themes, evolution, representative work, and academic influence without becoming a CV or literature review.
- Confirm slide 4 identifies which research themes deserve commercial attention and slide 5 states the recommendation.
- Confirm customer pain, buyer, current solution, and purchase logic appear before detailed methods, paper summaries, or technical architecture.
- Export the core-slide titles and confirm they form a coherent progression from scholar whole picture to research priorities, commercial recommendation, customer case, execution, and next decision.
- Confirm every academic-influence metric names its database and retrieval date and is not used as evidence of customer demand.
- Confirm an MBA-level generalist can identify within five minutes what to pursue, who pays, what is sold, why the customer switches, why this team can win, what could stop the case, and what happens in the next 90 days.
- Confirm the final PPT slide facilitates a decision or scholar discussion rather than ending generically.
- Deliver only PPT and Excel unless the user explicitly requested another format.
